ShaderToy is a website that allows you to run GLSL on the web for simple shaders and acts like a social media site where you can share said shaders.

I used ShaderToy to learn shader programming, exploring various graphics concepts.

Here are some of my favorite shaders I made.


Parallax Occlusion Mapping

An exploration of parallax occlusion mapping utilizing raymarching.


Height & Normal Canvas

An exploration of persistent buffers.


3D Reflections/Lighting Test

An exploration of raymarching to create shadows and reflections.


Desert Scene

An exploration of realistic raymarched scenes.


Tungsten Cube Recursion

An exploration of time and light warping.


4D Raymarcher

An exploration of transformations in four spatial dimensions.


Spatial Repetition/Rotation Test

An exploration of spatial repetition and rotation matrices.


Stylized Lens Flare

An exploration of advanced visual effects utilizing vector operations.


Wallpaper Style Hexagon Grid

An exploration of hexagonal grids.
