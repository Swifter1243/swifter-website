Extra Sensory 2 was a community ran live event for Beat Games' Critically acclaimed game Beat Saber.

A recording of someone's experience playing EXSII.

The event was a premiere of two new Beat Saber mods, Vivify and Synapse.

Vivify is a mod that adds new functionality to custom levels, allowing mappers to utilize Unity's Asset Bundles to instantiate prefabs, replace gameplay elements, apply post processing effects, and much more.

Synapse is a mod that allows creators to host and configure networked events, managing mod installations for any players who want to join. They can then compete on a configured set of custom levels in real time.

The team spent 3 years creating roughly 40 minutes of custom level content utilizing Vivify to premiere to the community through Synapse.

My Contributions to EXSII

My level for the event, titled "you", has quickly become a community favorite. In a short amount of time, it became the highest rated map on the community's custom level hosting site BeatSaver.

Every visual element was completely created by me, whether it be modeling, animating, or custom shaders.

The project has been completely open sourced as a reference for future mappers using Vivify.

The menu takeover in a player's game

I created a Main Menu takeover that shows up when you first launch Beat Saber, in order to promote the event with a huge countdown.

This asset references many of the custom levels from the event, and required animation and shader work from me.

I had to work closely with the programming of Synapse in order to properly integrate functionality of the asset, such as the countdown.

I also created an intro/outro sequence for the event.

The environment work for the intro was done by Mawntee, but the subtitle system, vocal processing, and animations were all created by me. The subtitle animations were generated and parsed from a file I wrote containing line timings.

For the outro, I produced an original track and created the entire sequence myself. The credits required shader work as well as a C# script to generate prefabs from a .txt file.

In order for the team and future mappers to easily create custom levels with Vivify, I created a Unity tool called VivifyTemplate.

This tool introduces an intuitive build window in order to streamline building Asset Bundles for your level. It handles multi-version headaches seamlessly, allowing mappers to develop for multiple platforms in one project.

It also comes with an assortment of utilities/examples for creators to utilize and reference.

Being a lead developer on the project, I took initiative in project management and organization.

I often convened with members, discussed goals, and outlined our plans for the coming days/weeks. I often went into other projects in order to pick up slack to keep things moving along.

Eventually, I even set up a JIRA project to manage the workload as we were approaching release.

Extra Sensory II was the most successful event in Beat Saber history, peaking at around 2000 concurrent players. The player count created a spike in the game's steam charts.
