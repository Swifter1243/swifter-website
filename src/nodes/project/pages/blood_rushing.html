BRLCTAP is a 40 minute experience I put together in Beat Saber over the course of a year using my own tools.

A playthrough of BRLCTAP.

BRLCTAP ("Blood Rushing Like Current Through a Powerline") is an album made by my friend acloudyskye. It has been an extremely influential album to me artistically, so I knew I wanted to create experiences using it's songs.

Somewhere Out There, released on November 30th, 2021.

The first experience I created was for the track Somewhere Out There, which was my favorite of the album.

Me and my friend Reddek were greatly enjoying this track at the time, so we created a rough storyboard for what we wanted in the level. He worked on some of the early parts, and my friend Mawntee hopped on to do animations for the blocks you hit.

I had been working on some technology to turn Blender shapes into Beat Saber objects, and I wanted to use this map to introduce the community to the potential of this technology. This map was really the map where I wanted to prove myself to the community.

Needless to say it worked and became the highest rated level of all time, also winning "Modchart of the Year" and "Map of the Year" at the community's award event. It was also met with overwhelmingly positive reviews on it's BeatSaver page.

Somewhere Out There inspired the creation of ReMapper, which would be the tool I would use to create the rest of the maps going forward.


Heliov, released on July 11th, 2022.

Next up I wanted to create a map for the track Heliov.

I wanted to create a more intentional atmosphere this time; that of a dark, post-apocolypic, brutalist and oppressive atmosphere where the sense of wonder came from how much larger everything was than you. I also wanted to pack 5x more content into the runtime to make the world feel more connected and lived-in.

It was the most technically complex map I made up until that point, with animated environments introducing many optimization problems that had to work in tandem with other challenges, such as changing fog heights and never moving the player in world space.

The response to Heliov was divisive, with some preferring the colorful spectacle of Somewhere Out There and others preferring the scale and ambition of Heliov.

After that point, I realized I wanted to turn the entire album into an experience.

I knew I wasn't going to be able to do a full blown music video for every single track, so I aimed to do at least a unique environment with a lightshow for each track, with a few exceptions/surprises.

Someone named Shark already created a map for the track Downfall, so I merged my environment into their map.
