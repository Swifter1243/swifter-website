PlasmaShift is a game developed by me and my friends Evan and Josh in 48 hours for a game jam.

A short run of PlasmaShift.


You are a white blood cell and a foreign virus has attacked your host. Your job is to eradicate the virus to the best of your ability.

One thing I took on for this project was creating a lot of visual effects to make the game feel interesting to look at.

An example of an effect I worked on was this "bullet" sprite that comes from the alien virus. I wrote the shader myself using HLSL.

Another fun effect I made were these alien cells. I used voronoi noise and some nice colors in a custom shader to make them feel very vibrant and odd.

This cell (lovingly nicknamed 'Jeff'), also utilizes a fun cube shuffle animation I made using a script.

I also created all background elements. The background is composed of a fleshy 'sky' layer, as well as two foreground layers which move at different paces in order to create a sense of scale from the parallax.

The vein models I created are endlessly being spawned, moved, and discarded when they head offscreen to allow the game to feel like it's progressing forever.

As a fun experiment, I decided to implement the boids algorithm into the movement of the cells in the background.

I use Unity's collider system to create a 'neighborhood' for the cell, reducing the complexity of each individual boid.

In addition to three rules of the algorithm (separation, alignment, and cohesion) the cells will steer away from the borders of the screen.
